\begin{slide}{Multiple Scattering Example: Cp-MnCO{$\mathrm{{}_3}$}}

     {Cp-MnCO{$\mathrm{{}_3}$} =
       tricarbonyl($\eta^5$-cyclopentadienyl)manganese(I)}

    This molecule has linear Mn-C-O bonds, and two distinct
    Mn-C distances.

    \vspace{3mm}
    \begin{tabular}{ll}
      \begin{minipage}{57mm}

        \vspace{1mm}

        To model the EXAFS, we need these paths:

        \begin{tabular}{ll}
          \multicolumn{2}{l}{\Red{ring}} \\
          &  5 Mn-C at $\sim 2.13\,\rm \AA $ \\
          \multicolumn{2}{l}{\Red{carbonyl}} \\
          & 3 Mn-C at $\sim 1.78\,\rm \AA $  \\
          & 3 Mn-O at $\sim 2.93\,\rm \AA $  \\
          & 6 Mn-C-O paths at $\sim 2.93\,\rm \AA $  \\
          & 3 Mn-C-O-C paths at $\sim 2.93\,\rm \AA $  \\
        \end{tabular}

      \end{minipage}
      &
      \begin{minipage}{40mm} {\wgraph{38mm}{molecules/mnco3}} \end{minipage}\\
    \end{tabular}

    \vspace{2mm}

    The Multiple Scattering Paths will overlap the longer Mn-C(ring)
    distance!!

    \onslide+<2->
    Using only the single-scattering paths will gives:

    \begin{cenpage}{99mm}
      \begin{itemize}
      \item large coordination numbers: ~6 Mn-C(ring) at $2.10\,\rm \AA $,
        ~10 Mn-C(carbonyl) at $1.80\,\rm \AA $ , and ~10 Mn-O (carbonyl) at
        $2.99\,\rm \AA $.
      \item $E_0$ of ~ -13eV (pretty big! A hint!).
      \end{itemize}
    \end{cenpage}
 \vfill
 \end{slide}
